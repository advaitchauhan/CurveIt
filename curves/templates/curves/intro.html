About PrincetonCurveIt!

What is Princeton CurveIt?

Princeton CurveIt is a crowdsourced grade distribution service that allows students to search for and view grade curves for classes offered at Princeton. Grade distributions are available across multiple semesters for individual courses, departments, and professors.

Where does the data come from?

Every semester students must enter grades for at least 3 classes they took during the previous semester (except for freshmen). Data collection is completely anonymous - we do not store grades for any individual student. When a grade is entered, it is immediately added to a course's distribution, with no record of the student it came from.

Who has access?

This site is protected via Princeton CAS, which is also used to protect Blackboard and most of Princeton’s TigerApps. Only current Princeton students who have submitted at least 3 grades for the previous semester (or who are freshmen) can see the grade distributions.

Who made this?

Princeton CurveIt was created as a project for COS 333; Advanced Programming Techniques, by Suraj Bhat ‘16, Advait Chauhan ‘17, Garrett Gosse ‘16, Tyler Hoffman ‘17, and Sylvia Okafor ’16. We know how important grades are to students at Princeon, and that grade data is not public information. We think that CurveIt will enhance the course selection process, and will also give insight into the effect that the recent decision by the Grading Committee has had on individual courses, professors, and departments.

Special Permissions

If you are having trouble logging in, or were not enrolled in three classes at Princeton last semester, please contact us at princetoncurveit@gmail.com to receive permission to use the site.
